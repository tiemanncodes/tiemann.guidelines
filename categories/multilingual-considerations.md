# Multilingual Annotation Considerations

---

## Core Challenge

Meaning is not always preserved across languages.

---

## Key Dimensions

### 1. Semantic Equivalence
Does the translated content preserve the original meaning?

### 2. Cultural Context
Is the interpretation culturally appropriate?

### 3. Tone and Formality
Does tone shift across languages?

### 4. Ambiguity
Does translation introduce or remove ambiguity?

---

## Common Issues

- False cognates
- Tone mismatch (formal vs. informal)
- Intent distortion
- Over-literal translation

---

## Evaluation Strategy

- Analyze original intent first
- Compare meaning, not wording
- Identify shifts in tone or implication

---

## Decision Principle

Annotation must reflect intended meaning, not literal translation.
