# Spam and Deceptive Content

---

## Definition

Content designed to mislead, manipulate, or exploit users for clicks, data, or financial gain.

---

## Common Patterns

### 1. Unrealistic Offers
- Extreme discounts (e.g., 90% off)
- "Too good to be true" pricing

### 2. Urgency Manipulation
- "Only today"
- "Limited time"
- Excessive punctuation (!!!)

### 3. Lack of Transparency
- No identifiable seller
- No contact or verification

### 4. Clickbait Language
- Emotional triggers
- Misleading promises

---

## Evaluation Strategy

- Cross-check plausibility
- Identify manipulation signals
- Prioritize user safety over engagement

---

## Edge Cases

- Legitimate promotions (must include verifiable context)
- New brands (require additional scrutiny)

---

## Decision Rule

If multiple spam signals are present → classify as Spam / Low Quality.
