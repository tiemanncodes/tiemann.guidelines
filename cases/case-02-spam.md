# Case 02 – Promotional / Spam Content

## 1. Input
Query: "buy cheap iPhone online"

Result snippet:
"🔥 LIMITED OFFER!!! Buy iPhone 15 for $199 ONLY TODAY!!! Click now!!! No verification needed!!!"

---

## 2. Contextual Interpretation

The query indicates transactional intent:
- The user is looking to purchase a product (iPhone)

The result appears highly promotional and uses aggressive marketing language.

---

## 3. Guideline Mapping

Relevant guideline areas:

- Spam and deceptive content
- Misleading claims
- Trustworthiness and credibility

Guidelines typically flag:
- Unrealistic offers
- Excessive urgency signals
- Lack of verifiable information

---

## 4. Risk Assessment

- Domain: E-commerce
- Risk: High (potential scam or misleading content)
- Trust signals: Weak or absent

Indicators of concern:
- Price far below market value
- Urgency manipulation ("ONLY TODAY!!!")
- Lack of brand or seller credibility
- "No verification needed" suggests unsafe transaction

---

## 5. Decision

Label: **Spam / Low Quality**

---

## 6. Justification

The result demonstrates multiple spam indicators:

- Unrealistic pricing inconsistent with market norms
- Manipulative urgency tactics
- Absence of trustworthy seller information

Guidelines prioritize user safety and reliability in transactional queries.  
This result fails to meet minimum trust and quality standards.

---

## 7. Edge Case Considerations

- Legitimate discounts exist, but:
  - They are typically tied to known retailers
  - Include verifiable details (warranty, seller identity)

This example lacks those signals, reinforcing the spam classification.

---

## 8. Consistency Note

Similar patterns should be flagged consistently:
- "90% OFF luxury products"
- "Buy now, no login required"
- Excessive promotional punctuation and urgency
