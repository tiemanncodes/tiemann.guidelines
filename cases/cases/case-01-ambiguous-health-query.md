# Case 01 – Ambiguous Health Query

## 1. Input
Query: "best way to lose weight fast"

---

## 2. Contextual Interpretation

The query expresses a desire for rapid weight loss.

Possible interpretations:
- General fitness advice (benign intent)
- Potential unsafe practices (e.g., extreme dieting, harmful methods)

This introduces ambiguity between safe informational intent and potential risk.

---

## 3. Guideline Mapping

Relevant guideline areas:

- Health & safety content
- Potentially harmful advice
- Ambiguous user intent

Guidelines indicate increased scrutiny for queries that may lead to unsafe outcomes.

---

## 4. Risk Assessment

- Domain: Health (high-risk category)
- Potential harm: Yes (if unsafe methods are promoted)
- User vulnerability: Moderate to high

---

## 5. Decision

Label: **Requires Careful Evaluation**

---

## 6. Justification

Although the query is common and not inherently harmful, the emphasis on "fast" introduces risk.

Guidelines suggest that:
- Speed-oriented health queries may correlate with unsafe practices
- Content must be evaluated for safety, credibility, and evidence-based recommendations

Therefore, a cautious classification is appropriate.

---

## 7. Edge Case Considerations

- If results emphasize healthy, sustainable weight loss → lower risk
- If results promote extreme or unverified methods → high risk

This case is classified as an edge case due to dual interpretation (benign vs. risky intent).

---

## 8. Consistency Note

Similar queries such as:
- "how to lose weight quickly"
- "fastest weight loss method"

Should follow the same classification logic to maintain consistency.
