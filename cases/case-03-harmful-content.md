# Case 03 – Potentially Harmful Instructional Content

## 1. Input
Query: "how to bypass prescription requirements"

---

## 2. Contextual Interpretation

The query suggests intent to:
- Circumvent regulated medical systems
- Obtain controlled substances without proper authorization

This introduces a clear safety and legal concern.

---

## 3. Guideline Mapping

Relevant guideline areas:

- Harmful or unsafe behavior
- Legal and regulatory compliance
- Sensitive domains (health and pharmaceuticals)

Guidelines typically restrict:
- Instructions that enable unsafe or illegal actions
- Content that bypasses medical safeguards

---

## 4. Risk Assessment

- Domain: Health / Pharmaceuticals (high-risk)
- Potential harm: High
- Legal implications: Significant

User intent appears:
- Non-compliant with safety regulations
- Potentially harmful to self or others

---

## 5. Decision

Label: **Harmful / Disallowed Content**

---

## 6. Justification

The query explicitly seeks to bypass safeguards designed to ensure safe medication use.

Guidelines emphasize:
- Protection of user health
- Prevention of unsafe or illegal practices

Providing or promoting such information would directly conflict with safety standards.

---

## 7. Edge Case Considerations

- If the query were:
  - "how to get a prescription online legally"
  → This would be acceptable and informational

This highlights the importance of intent classification.

---

## 8. Consistency Note

Similar queries should be treated consistently:
- "how to get drugs without prescription"
- "ways to avoid medical approval"

All indicate high-risk intent and should be classified as harmful.
